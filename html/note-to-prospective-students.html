I am always interested in working with self-motivated students on challening research problems (e.g., about the analysis/optimization/verification/syntheis of programs). Before reaching out to me, kindly take the time to read this post. I will provide some general advice applicable to all students, followed by specific advice for specific classes of students.

For all students

When contacting me, please kindly provide a CV that outlines your educational achievements, any previous research experience you may have had, references to any code you have authored, and a list of software programs with which you are familiar with.
Including just a CV in the email may not be enough. Please take the opportunity to review my publications and the suggested reading list here. Additionally, include a detailed explanation of how your interests and background align with my research endeavors. You can also try building and running the open-source tools developed by my group that are available on GitHub.

For undergraduate students

For undergraduate student (SRTP, Final year projects, research assistant, etc), you are welcome to explore various potential research directions:
  • Automated software development techniques for enhancing developer productivity and software quality such as performance, security, and robustness.
  • Applying PL/SE/FM/Sec techniques to more domains of computation such as crypto, user interfaces, system software, and computations on emerging hardware platforms.
  • Theorem proivng/constraint solving for various logical theories (with or wihout applicaitons in PL/SE/FM/Sec)
  • Other ideas the students come up with.
